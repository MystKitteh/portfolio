Here is a recipe for homemade ramen:

Ingredients:
- 8 cups chicken or vegetable broth 
- 1 package ramen noodles
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon grated ginger
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon sesame oil
- Sliced cooked chicken breast or pork (optional)  
- Scallions, sliced thin
- Soft boiled egg (optional)

Instructions:

1. In a large pot, bring the broth to a boil over high heat. Add the ramen noodles and cook for 2-3 minutes until just tender. 

2. Add the garlic, ginger, soy sauce and sesame oil. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 5 more minutes.

3. Divide noodles and broth between 4 bowls. Top each bowl with sliced chicken or pork, scallions, soft boiled egg and any other desired toppings.

Some other tasty add-ins for homemade ramen are:
- Shiitake mushrooms
- Baby spinach or bok choy
- Fresh cilantro 
- Chili oil or chili paste
- Fresh lime juice

You can customize your ramen by adding different toppings and ingredients to match your tastes. Homemade ramen is so flavorful and satisfying! Enjoy!

Here is a recipe for homemade ramen:

Ingredients:
- 8 cups chicken or vegetable broth 
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp rice vinegar
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp freshly grated ginger 
- 2 boneless skinless chicken breasts or firm tofu, sliced
- 4 oz shiitake mushrooms, sliced 
- 2 cups baby spinach
- 8 oz ramen noodles
- 4 eggs, soft boiled
- Scallions, sliced for garnish
- Nori sheets, cut into strips for garnish

Instructions:

1. In a large pot, combine the broth, soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, garlic and ginger. Bring to a boil over high heat.

2. Add the chicken (or tofu), mushrooms and spinach. Reduce heat to medium and simmer for 5 minutes. 

3. Meanwhile, cook the ramen noodles according to package directions. Drain and set aside. 

4. Soft boil the eggs by placing in a pot of boiling water for 6 minutes. Cool under cold running water, peel and set aside.

5. To serve, place a portion of the noodles into a bowl. Ladle the hot broth over the top. Top with slices of chicken (or tofu), mushrooms, spinach, soft boiled egg and garnishes like scallions and nori. Enjoy!

You can customize this recipe by adding other proteins like pork or shrimp, using different types of noodles, or topping with extras like kimchi, bean sprouts or chili oil. Play around with the ingredients to make it your own!

Here is a basic recipe for homemade ramen:

Ingredients:
- 8 cups chicken or vegetable broth
- 1 package ramen noodles
- 3 tbsp soy sauce
- 2 tbsp mirin (sweet rice wine)
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 inch ginger, grated 
- 3 green onions, sliced
- soft boiled eggs (optional)
- nori sheets, sliced (optional)

Instructions:
1. In a large pot, bring the broth to a boil over high heat. Add the ramen noodles and cook for 2-3 minutes until al dente. 

2. In a small bowl, combine the soy sauce, mirin, sesame oil, garlic and ginger. Stir to mix well.

3. Remove the pot from the heat. Stir in the soy sauce mixture. Add the green onions.

4. Ladle the ramen and broth into bowls. Top with soft boiled egg and nori if desired.

5. Serve immediately and enjoy!

You can customize this basic ramen recipe by adding other toppings like shredded pork, mushrooms, spinach, corn, etc. I suggest using good quality broth and fresh ingredients to make tasty homemade ramen. Let me know if you try this recipe!

Here is a recipe for homemade ramen:

Ingredients:
- 8 cups chicken or vegetable broth 
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
- 1 teaspoon sesame oil
- 1 tablespoon grated ginger
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 8 ounces ramen noodles
- Thinly sliced cooked pork, beef, chicken or extra firm tofu (optional)
- Chopped scallions, boiled eggs, nori sheets for serving (optional)

Instructions:

1. In a large pot, combine the broth, soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, ginger and garlic. Bring to a boil over high heat.

2. Add the ramen noodles and cook for 2-3 minutes, until al dente. 

3. Divide the noodles and broth between 4 large soup bowls. Top each serving with sliced meat or tofu, if using. 

4. Garnish with scallions, boiled egg halves or slices, and nori sheets. Serve immediately.

Some variations:

- For a spicy miso ramen, add 1-2 tablespoons of miso paste and 1 teaspoon of chili oil or paste to the broth. 

- For a creamy tonkotsu ramen, use an umami-rich pork or chicken bone broth instead of plain broth.

- Add sautéed mushrooms, baby spinach, corn or bamboo shoots for extra veggies.

- Top with seasoned soft boiled eggs, wood ear mushrooms, roasted seaweed or marinated bamboo shoots.

- Drizzle with a flavored oil like chili oil, sesame oil or garlic oil.

Let me know if you try this homemade ramen recipe! Enjoy experimenting and customizing it to your taste.
